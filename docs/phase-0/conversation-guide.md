# Phase 0 — 20-Minute Conversation Guide

**You are doing customer research, not selling.** Your only goal in each call is to learn. The single best signal you'll get is whether they'd hand you money — but only if you earn the answer through real listening, not by pitching.

**Best practice:** record the call (with permission) so you can transcribe and review later. The Otter / Fireflies workflow is fine. Your memory is not reliable across 10 calls.

---

## Pre-call (5 min, day before)

Look up:
- Their last 12 months of transaction volume (MLS, Zillow profile)
- Their team structure (solo, team of 3, team of 12)
- Their brokerage
- One personal hook — recent podcast, listing, anniversary

Write 3 sentences in the tracker before the call so you go in prepared.

---

## Opening (2 min)

> "Thanks for the time. Quick context — I run a brokerage in Vegas. We built an internal TC system because we couldn't find one that worked the way we wanted. Before I think about making it available to anyone else, I'm talking to 10 producing agents about their TC reality. **No pitch, no demo, no follow-up sequence. Just 20 minutes of your perspective.** Sound good?"

Set the stakes. Reduce defensiveness. Then shut up.

---

## Discovery (15 min)

Ask these in order. Don't read them — internalize them. Listen for the *unsaid* parts. Probe with "tell me more" / "what do you mean by that" / "give me an example."

### 1. Their workflow today
> "Walk me through how a transaction goes from accepted offer to closed for you. Who does what?"

**Listen for:** chaos, manual handoffs, dropped balls, what they personally still touch vs. what's delegated. Where does "the system" fail and they fix it manually?

### 2. Their TC reality
> "Who handles the actual coordination work — opening escrow, chasing signatures, sending the right emails on the right day?"

Possible answers:
- "Me, mostly" → solo agent doing it themselves (huge pain, time sink)
- "My assistant" → in-house TC (worried about cost / turnover)
- "We use [Service Name] / a 1099 TC" → outsourced (worried about quality / cost / lack of control)
- "Our brokerage handles it" → brokerage-provided (probably underserved)

### 3. The cost
> "What does that cost you? Either in dollars or in your own hours."

**The number you want to hear:** $150–$500/file for a 1099 TC service. Or "I spend 6–8 hours per file myself." Either tells you they're feeling the pain.

### 4. The pain
> "What's the most frustrating part of TC for you specifically?"

Common answers:
- "Files slip through the cracks" → workflow visibility problem
- "I never know what's outstanding" → daily-task visibility problem
- "My TC is great but I can't see what they're doing" → transparency / trust problem
- "I waste hours coordinating between agents and escrow" → communication problem
- "I forgot to send X email and lost a referral" → automation problem

Each of these maps to a different feature priority. Magenta TC is strong on workflow visibility + automated emails — make sure those are real pains for them.

### 5. The wishlist
> "If you could wave a magic wand and have the perfect TC system, what would it do that nothing today does?"

This is gold. Their wishlist becomes your roadmap. Push for specificity: "what would the screen show on a Tuesday morning?"

### 6. Other tools tried
> "Have you tried other TC tools? What didn't work?"

Listen for: Skyslope, Dotloop, Brokermint, Folio, Open To Close, transactly, kvCORE TC modules. The reasons they bounced are your competitive advantages.

### 7. Pricing probe (this is the critical one)
> "If I built a TC system that did [the 2 things they said hurt most] — automated daily checklist, email automation, real visibility into every file — what would you pay per month for it, unlimited files?"

**Wait for the number.** Don't fill the silence. Whatever they say is your data.

Common answers and what they mean:
- **"$50–$100/mo"** → solo agent budget, you're in-range
- **"$200–$500/mo"** → team budget, very strong signal
- **"Depends on what it does"** → soft no — they don't have enough pain
- **"I'd need to see it"** → not a buyer yet, but maybe later
- **"I already pay $X/file, so..."** → they're doing the math right there. Their per-file × monthly volume is your ceiling.

If the number feels low, push gently:
> "What if it replaced your current TC service entirely?" or
> "What if it cut the time you spend on TC in half?"

You're calibrating their ceiling.

### 8. Design partner ask
> "Would you be willing to be a design partner — use it free for 90 days before launch in exchange for shaping it as I build?"

A "yes" here is the strongest signal you'll get all call. Free is easy to say yes to, but they only commit if they actually want this thing.

**Get 2–3 design partner commitments out of 12 conversations and you have product-market fit signal.**

### 9. Referrals
> "Who else should I be talking to? Who do you know that runs an efficient team or has strong opinions about TC?"

Names = warm intros = next round of conversations.

---

## Wrap (1 min)

> "Massively appreciate the time. I'll send you a brief follow-up note thanking you, and a link to a 1-pager on what I'm building if you want to look. **No drip campaign, no sales calls — promise.** I'll let you know how the project evolves if you want."

That last line ("if you want") respects their time. Don't auto-add them to anything.

---

## After the call (10 min, same day)

While it's fresh, log into the tracker and fill in:

| Field | What to capture |
|---|---|
| Top pain | Their actual words. Quote them. |
| Currently uses | Tool name + cost |
| Pays today | $/file or $/mo |
| WTP | The number they said |
| Design partner? | Y / N / Maybe |
| Referrals | Names + how to reach |
| Notes | Anything that surprised you, any "huh, didn't expect that" moments |
| Status | Cold (no reply) / Warm (took the call) / Hot (committed design partner) |

Then **send a 4-line thank-you email**:

> Hey [name] — thanks for the time. The two things you said about [specific pain] really stuck with me. If you want, here's a 1-pager I put together: [link]. I'll keep you posted as it evolves. — Brendan

That's it. No drip. No CTA.

---

## At the end of 10 conversations — the validation memo

Write a 1-page memo answering these questions with quotes from the calls:

1. **Is the pain real?** What % of conversations had someone describe genuine TC pain?
2. **Is it worth solving?** What's the median willingness-to-pay number?
3. **What 3 features did prospects rank highest?** (Use the wishlist + magic-wand answers)
4. **Who are the 2–3 design partners?** Names + commitments.
5. **Are competitors strong or weak?** What % said "I love [Skyslope/Dotloop/etc.]"?
6. **Decision:** GO (build) or NO-GO (stay on polished-Sheets path)?

This memo is what you use to decide whether to commit 18 months of your life. Take it seriously.

---

## Honest anti-patterns to watch for

These are reasons you'd think you have signal but actually don't:

- **"Sounds great, send me a demo when it's ready"** = polite no. Real interest sounds like "if you build that I'll switch from [current]."
- **Friends and family enthusiasm** = noise. They like you, not the product.
- **Vague "yes I'd pay something"** without a number = noise. Always push for the number.
- **Top-1% agents only** = sample bias. They have systems. The pain is in the middle 60%.
- **"I'd love to use it but my brokerage decides"** = wrong audience. You wanted the team lead who actually decides.

A real signal looks like: *"If this had [feature X] and cost less than $[Y], I'd switch from [current] tomorrow. Here's a referral. When can I be a design partner?"* Two of those across 12 calls = green light. Five = run.
