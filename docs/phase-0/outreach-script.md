# Phase 0 — Cold Outreach Scripts

**Goal:** book 12 conversations with solo top agents + team leads.
**NOT goal:** sell anything, demo anything, or convince anyone Magenta TC is great.

You're asking for **20 minutes of their time** in exchange for genuine curiosity about their business. People say yes to that more than you think — especially other agents.

---

## Script 1 — LinkedIn DM (preferred channel for cold)

> **Hi [first name],**
>
> Quick one — I'm Brendan, run a Vegas brokerage and built an internal transaction-coordination system that's saved our team a ton of hours. Thinking about making it available to other agents and teams.
>
> Before I build it for anyone but us, I want to talk to ~10 producing agents about their TC pain — what works, what sucks, what they wish existed. **Not pitching anything.** Just want to know if I'm building the right thing.
>
> Got 20 min in the next two weeks for a no-pressure call? I'll buy you a virtual coffee.
>
> — Brendan

**Why it works:** specific (20 min, two weeks), low-stakes ("not pitching"), credentials light ("run a Vegas brokerage" — they can verify), genuine ask. No links, no attachments — those trigger spam filters.

## Script 2 — Email (use when you have their address)

**Subject:** `quick question for a fellow agent — Brendan from Magenta`

> Hey [first name],
>
> Found you through [LinkedIn / a mutual / your brokerage's site / Bigger Pockets / etc.]. I'm Brendan King — own a brokerage in Las Vegas (King Vegas Homes / Magenta TC).
>
> Last year we built our own transaction-coordination system because we couldn't find one that fit how we actually work. It's saved us hours per file. I'm now thinking about offering it to other producing agents and teams.
>
> Before I build a single feature for anyone outside our team, I want to talk to 10 agents about their TC workflow — what they pay, what works, what frustrates them. **No pitch, no demo, no follow-up sequence.** Just 20 minutes of your perspective.
>
> Open to a quick call in the next two weeks? I'm flexible on timing.
>
> — Brendan
> [your phone] | [your scheduling link if you have one]

## Script 3 — Warm intro request (when asking a mutual)

If someone you trust knows the prospect, this 5x's your reply rate.

**To the mutual:**

> Hey [name],
>
> Asking a small favor. I'm doing customer research for a TC software product I'm thinking about building (basically what I built for our internal team, productized). I'm trying to talk to 10 producing agents and team leads to validate the idea.
>
> Would you mind double-tapping me into [prospect name]? They're exactly the kind of agent I want to learn from. Just need 20 min of their time — not a pitch.
>
> Happy to introduce you to anyone I know who might help you, anytime.
>
> — Brendan

**Then a "double opt-in" forwarded note** the mutual can send:

> [Mutual's intro] —
>
> Hey [prospect], meet Brendan King — runs a brokerage in Vegas, building a TC tool. He's looking to interview a few producing agents about their TC workflow — no pitch, just learning. Thought you two should know each other.
>
> [Mutual]

## Where to find your 12

**Solo top agents (target 6):**
- Local Realtor Association directory — sort by transaction volume if available
- Realtor.com / Zillow agent listings in your market — top "Recently Sold" counts
- LinkedIn search: `Realtor` + city + "30+ deals" or "$30M+"
- Bigger Pockets / Inman / RealTrends top-1000 lists
- People you've co-listed with or worked across the table from in the last 2 years

**Team leads (target 6):**
- Look for `[Name] Team` or `[Name] Group` in your MLS
- Compass / Side / RE/MAX team rosters in nearby markets
- LinkedIn: search `Real Estate Team Lead` + state
- Top-producing teams from Inman + RealTrends rankings
- Your own broker network — ask who runs the most efficient teams

**Avoid in Phase 0:**
- Brokerage owners (Phase 5 audience)
- Agents under 12 deals/year (not enough volume to feel TC pain)
- Agents in markets very different from yours (different workflow norms)
- Friends and family (they'll tell you what you want to hear)

## Cadence

- **Week 1**: send 12 messages on Mon/Tue. Spread across LinkedIn (8) + email (4).
- **Week 1 Thu**: send polite bumps to non-responders.
- **Week 2**: 4–6 calls.
- **Week 3**: 4–6 more calls + start compiling notes.
- **Week 4**: final calls + write the 1-page validation memo.

**Reply rate to expect:** 20–35% on cold LinkedIn DMs, 30–50% on warm intros, 15–25% on cold email. So 12 messages → ~3–5 conversations. **Send 24 messages if you want 12 calls.**

---

## What NOT to do

- ❌ Don't send a deck. You're not pitching.
- ❌ Don't include a Calendly link in the first message — feels transactional.
- ❌ Don't mention pricing in the cold message.
- ❌ Don't ghost on the bump message — be warm and brief.
- ❌ Don't ask for an NDA. You're learning, not exposing trade secrets.
- ❌ Don't follow up more than twice. If they don't reply, move on.

## What TO do

- ✅ Personalize the first sentence — mention their team name, recent listing, podcast appearance
- ✅ Reference your real estate background — that's your credibility, not your tech
- ✅ Be human and brief
- ✅ Track every conversation in the Phase 0 tab of the master sheet (see "tracker")
- ✅ Listen way more than talk on the actual call
